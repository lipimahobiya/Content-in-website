SOLAR SYSTEM

Mercury

Mercury, the smallest and innermost planet in our solar system, is a rocky world with a cratered surface similar to Earth's Moon. It orbits the sun rapidly, completing a year in just 88 Earth days, and its day-night cycle is unusually long, taking 59 Earth days. Mercury's extreme temperatures, ranging from scorching highs to frigid lows, are due to its lack of a substantial atmosphere.

Venus

Venus, the second planet from the sun, is Earth's closest planetary neighbor and is known as the "Earth's twin" due to its similar size and mass. It's also the hottest planet in our solar system, with a surface temperature hot enough to melt lead, and is shrouded in a thick, toxic atmosphere of carbon dioxide and sulfuric acid clouds.

Earth

Earth, our home planet, is the third from the Sun and the only known celestial body to support life, often called the "blue planet" due to its abundant water. It's a dynamic sphere, characterized by a rocky crust, a molten mantle, and a solid inner core. Earth's atmosphere, composed primarily of nitrogen and oxygen, protects it from harmful solar radiation and sustains life. Our planet's rotation causes day and night, while its orbit around the sun defines seasons and the year. The Earth's unique combination of factors, including its distance from the sun, its atmosphere, and the presence of water, makes it a habitable haven in the vastness of space.

Mars

Mars, the fourth planet from the Sun, is often called the "Red Planet" due to its rusty-red surface, caused by iron oxide dust. It's a cold, rocky, and dry world with a thin atmosphere, and its surface is marked by canyons, volcanoes, and craters. Mars has two moons, Phobos and Deimos, and a day on Mars is slightly longer than Earth's, while a year is almost twice as long. Scientists are intrigued by the possibility of past or even present life on Mars, given its potential for past water and similarities to Earth.

Jupiter

Jupiter, the largest planet in our solar system and a gas giant, is known for its swirling cloud stripes and the Great Red Spot, a massive storm that has raged for centuries. It's the fifth planet from the sun and is composed primarily of hydrogen and helium, with no solid surface, and is twice as massive as all other planets combined.

Saturn

Saturn, the sixth planet from the sun and the second-largest in our solar system, is a gas giant known for its spectacular ring system, which is made of ice and dust particles. It's also the farthest planet visible to the naked eye and is named after the Roman god of agriculture.
